\section{Introduction}
This document presents side-by-side comparison grids (1x2 layout) of Byzantine robustness results comparing Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) with Direct encoding on MNIST, extended up to $f=10$ Byzantine clients. The configurations compared are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{CNN}: Convolutional Neural Network on MNIST (`plots\_cnn\_vs\_snn\_direct\_f10/cnn`)
    \item \textbf{SNN Direct}: Spiking Neural Network with Direct encoding on MNIST (`plots\_cnn\_vs\_snn\_direct\_f10/snn\_direct`)
\end{enumerate}

Each grid illustrates the test accuracy under varying numbers of Byzantine clients ($f \in [0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10]$) and data heterogeneity levels ($\gamma \in [0.0, 0.33, 0.66, 1.0]$) using a single training seed (seed 42), evaluated at the validation-based best step.
